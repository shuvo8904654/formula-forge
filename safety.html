Lab Safety Reference

Essential safety information for working with chemicals

Back to Periodic Table

Highly Dangerous Elements

  • Fluorine (F) - Extremely reactive, toxic gas. Can cause severe burns.
  • Chlorine (Cl) - Toxic gas, respiratory irritant. Used in WWI as chemical weapon.
  • Mercury (Hg) - Toxic heavy metal. Vapors are dangerous. Causes neurological damage.
  • Lead (Pb) - Cumulative poison. Causes developmental issues, especially in children.
  • Arsenic (As) - Highly toxic. Historical poison of choice.
  • Thallium (Tl) - Extremely toxic. Once used as rat poison.
  • Plutonium (Pu) - Radioactive, toxic. Alpha emitter dangerous if inhaled.
  • Polonium (Po) - Intensely radioactive. Used in assassination of Alexander Litvinenko.

Handle with Caution

  • Sodium (Na) - Reacts violently with water. Store under oil.
  • Potassium (K) - More reactive than sodium. Explosive with water.
  • Phosphorus (P) - White phosphorus ignites spontaneously in air.
  • Bromine (Br) - Corrosive liquid, toxic vapors. Causes severe burns.
  • Cesium (Cs) - Explodes on contact with water. Most reactive alkali metal.
  • Beryllium (Be) - Dust is highly toxic. Causes berylliosis.
  • Cadmium (Cd) - Toxic heavy metal. Accumulates in kidneys.
  • Chromium (Cr) - Hexavalent chromium is carcinogenic.

General Lab Safety Rules

  • Always wear appropriate PPE: safety glasses, lab coat, gloves
  • Know the location of safety equipment: eyewash, shower, fire extinguisher
  • Never eat, drink, or apply cosmetics in the lab
  • Read Safety Data Sheets (SDS) before handling any chemical
  • Never smell chemicals directly - waft vapors toward nose
  • Add acid to water, never water to acid
  • Label all containers clearly
  • Dispose of chemicals properly - never down the drain
  • Work in a fume hood when handling volatile substances
  • Never work alone in the lab

Hazard Symbols

Flammable

Substances that can catch fire easily. Keep away from heat, sparks, and open flames.

Oxidizer

Can cause or intensify fire. Keep away from flammable materials.

Corrosive

Can cause severe skin burns and eye damage. Handle with appropriate protection.

Toxic

Can cause death or serious health effects. Avoid all contact and inhalation.

Irritant

May cause skin or eye irritation. Use appropriate protection.

Radioactive

Emits ionizing radiation. Requires special handling and shielding.
